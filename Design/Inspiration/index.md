<p>Here are some site features from various sites that might be good inspiration for our own site design.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adobe_Web_Platform_Team_Blog">Adobe Web Platform Team Blog</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://blogs.adobe.com/webplatform/">http://blogs.adobe.com/webplatform/</a>
</p><p>Nice body-text font, readable and friendly. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://font.ubuntu.com/">Ubuntu</a> typeface with font-size of 16px and line-height of 24px.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:AdobeBlog.png" class="image"><img alt="AdobeBlog.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/7/77/AdobeBlog.png" width="698" height="229" /></a>
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="VoilaVie.fr">VoilaVie.fr</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.voilavie.fr/view/2595">http://www.voilavie.fr/view/2595</a>
</p><p>This site has several nice features:
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shrinking_Header">Shrinking Header</span></h3>
<p>The header banner shrinks and retracts when the user scrolls down, changing the focus from the initial impression of site identity to an unobtrusive, utilitarian 
</p><p>Full:
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Voilavie-full.png" class="image"><img alt="Voilavie-full.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/4/47/Voilavie-full.png" width="1017" height="193" /></a>
</p><p>Scrolled:
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Voilavie-scrolled.png" class="image"><img alt="Voilavie-scrolled.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/0/09/Voilavie-scrolled.png" width="1017" height="140" /></a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animated_Scrolling_Gallery_Thumbnails">Animated Scrolling Gallery Thumbnails</span></h3>
<p>Each gallery thumbnail consists of a picture and a caption; when the item is scrolled over, the caption title turns red, and the picture scrolls up to reveal the caption description.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Voilavie-gallery.png" class="image"><img alt="Voilavie-gallery.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/7/7f/Voilavie-gallery.png" width="374" height="317" /></a> &#160;  &#160;  &#160; <a href="/wiki/File:Voilavie-gallery-mouseover.png" class="image"><img alt="Voilavie-gallery-mouseover.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/a/a3/Voilavie-gallery-mouseover.png" width="375" height="315" /></a>
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="BadassJS">BadassJS</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://badassjs.com/">http://badassjs.com/</a>
</p><p>A nice use of dropshadow on the content area, trendy floating folded banner, and subtle grid background to add texture.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Badassjs.png" class="image"><img alt="Badassjs.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/3/35/Badassjs.png" width="885" height="274" /></a>
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="HTML5Rocks">HTML5Rocks</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.html5rocks.com/">http://www.html5rocks.com/</a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_.26_Navigation">Search &amp; Navigation</span></h3>
<p>Nice use of icons in full-width menu dropdown to indicate different technology categories. Also, nice layout and colors on menus.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Html5rocks.png" class="image"><img alt="Html5rocks.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/9/97/Html5rocks.png" width="1200" height="144" /></a>
</p><p>Ability to filter list of articles by format, audience, technology or tags (would be nice to have other parameters, like date, author, etc.).
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Html5rocks-filters.png" class="image"><img alt="Html5rocks-filters.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/7/76/Html5rocks-filters.png" width="1027" height="536" /></a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile_Page_.2F_Bio">Profile Page / Bio</span></h3>
<p>Map of location, profile picture, list of contributions. (Our site should have a profile page for each contributor, but might have a "collection page" for all contributors in a team or employed by a specific organization.)
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Html5rocks-profile.png" class="image"><img alt="Html5rocks-profile.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/4/4d/Html5rocks-profile.png" width="1116" height="346" /></a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="DocHub">DocHub</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://dochub.io/">http://dochub.io/</a>
</p><p>Excellent autocomplete live-filtering search box. Simple, clear, non-distracting design.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Dochub.png" class="image"><img alt="Dochub.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/3/33/Dochub.png" width="717" height="718" /></a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ReadTheDocs">ReadTheDocs</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://readthedocs.org/docs/sublime-text-unofficial-documentation/en/latest/search_and_replace/search_and_replace.html">http://readthedocs.org/docs/sublime-text-unofficial-documentation/en/latest/search_and_replace/search_and_replace.html</a>
</p><p>Elided long strings on items in sidebar are revealed in full text when sidebar is moused over (or in focus), rather than using a horizontal scroll bar. Focused list item is outlined.
</p><p>For serial content, it's good to have a "previous" and "next" link, with page title, though it doesn't necessarily have to be in sidebar (could be in footer, or on right side callout, or whatever). 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Readthedocs-sidebar.png" class="image"><img alt="Readthedocs-sidebar.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/6/6f/Readthedocs-sidebar.png" width="340" height="453" /></a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Django_Book">The Django Book</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://djangobook.com/en/2.0/chapter02/">http://djangobook.com/en/2.0/chapter02/</a>
</p><p>Comments system lets you comment on any block inline in a handy but unobtrusive way, with a marker that indicates how many comments that block has. When the marker is activated, it brings up a forum-style comment dialog. 
</p><p>This could be implemented even more easily and extensibly by opening an absolute-positioned iframe with a dedicated forum page; the user could then choose to go directly to the full forum view, or could comment right there. It would also be nice if the comments were in the sidebar, rather than over the content area.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Djangobook-comments.png" class="image"><img alt="Djangobook-comments.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/5/5d/Djangobook-comments.png" width="600" height="424" /></a> &#160; <a href="/wiki/File:Djangobook-comments-open.png" class="image"><img alt="Djangobook-comments-open.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/a/a7/Djangobook-comments-open.png" width="600" height="424" /></a>
</p><p>Some details about the comment system are on the help menu.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Djangobook-about.png" class="image"><img alt="Djangobook-about.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/e/e4/Djangobook-about.png" width="932" height="695" /></a>
</p>

</p><p><a href="/wiki/File:Inset-code.png" class="image"><img alt="Inset-code.png" src="//static.webplatform.org/w/public/5/5f/Inset-code.png" width="441" height="407" /></a>
</p>

   }

</pre>
